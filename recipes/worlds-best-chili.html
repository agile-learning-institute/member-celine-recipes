Description

This recipe is sure to get meat lovers swooning over getting their veggies for the day. With a mix of beans, fire-roasted tomatoes, and some other delicious veggies, this reicpe is protein-packed and easy on the digestive tract. Meat eaters won't even realize this doesn't have meat in it when they are overwhelmed by the flavors of the dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 can of chickpeas
  • 1 can of black beans
  • 1 can of kidney beans (whichever you prefer)
  • 1 can of diced fire-roasted tomatoes
  • 1 white onion
  • 2 bell peppers
  • 3 jalapenos (with seeds removed)
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • Spices: Salt, pepper, chili powder, cumin, smoked garlic powder, and coriander

Steps

  1. Dice the onion, bell peppers, jalapenos, and garlic. If you have time, saute these in a pan then add them to the crockpot to make the chili more flavorful.
  2. Add the diced veggies, garlic, fire-roasted tomatoes, chickpeas, kidney beans, and black beans to the crockpot. Make sure to drain and rinse all of the beans. Do not drain the tomatoes.
  3. Use spices to season to taste. A generous sprinkle throught the chili of all the spices mentioned should do the trick.
  4. Stir all of the ingredients and cook the chili on high for 8 hours.
  5. After the chili is done, you can serve it with a dollop of yogurt or sour cream, cilantro, shredded cheese, green onion, or any other desired toppings!
